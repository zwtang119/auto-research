### F4 7-check details — reconstructed framing

**Check 1 — Single-methodological-insight sentence**: PASS. The abstract and §1 both carry the load-bearing sentence verbatim. Removing the corpus, the sentence remains a method claim ("predict which subset of verdicts will be reversed upon re-adjudication, using a reconciliation-loss signal derived from a dual-ledger crosswalk and a Murphy-decomposed Brier replay"). The corpus dependence is moved to substrate, not contribution.

**Check 2 — Removal-of-data-uniqueness survival**: PASS. The 22-investigation corpus is explicitly framed as the evaluation substrate: the method is testable on any audit-trail corpus that (a) admits a dual-ledger crosswalk, (b) carries a pre-reversal probability vector, and (c) has a realised re-adjudication record. The abstract states this verbatim. The contribution does not collapse when the 22-investigation corpus is removed; what collapses is the empirical magnitudes, which are honestly marked `[PLACEHOLDER-NOT-COMPUTED-YET]`. This is the check the original Y framing most decisively failed.

**Check 3 — Evaluative-claim articulation**: PASS. The evaluative claim is concrete: "the reconciliation-loss feature achieves 4-FICV AUROC of `[PLACEHOLDER-NOT-COMPUTED-YET]` on the 22-investigation verdicts containing $N_{gt}$ reversal events." The placeholder is a deliberate honesty device — the paper is a draft, not a paper-with-results — but the claim structure is articulated and the slots into which empirical magnitudes will fall are named. A reader knows what the paper will claim once the run is done.

**Check 4 — Knowledge-gap addressed**: PASS. The paper names the gap explicitly: harness bugs surface as reversals only after re-adjudication, and no prior method predicts the reversal-prone verdicts *before* re-adjudication occurs. The gap is the pre-reversal detection problem, stated as a function from $t_0$ features to a $y_i$ label that is only realisable at $t_1$.

**Check 5 — Transferability-of-insight**: PASS (marginal). The insight transfers to any LLM-as-judge harness whose audit trail admits the dual-ledger crosswalk structure. The marginality is honest: corpora meeting the three preconditions (dual-ledger crosswalk, pre-reversal probability vector, realised re-adjudication) are not yet common in the field, and the §5 limitations flag this explicitly. The method is transferable in principle; the population of substrates it can be tested on is currently narrow.

**Check 6 — Deep-concern resilience**: PASS. The deep concern raised against the original Y framing — "datasets-as-endpoints don't meet the bar on their own" (NeurIPS 2026 E&D) and "mostly a description of the corpus and its collection" (ACL/ARR canonical reject) — is named, quoted, and absorbed in §1 of the reconstruction. The reconstruction does not survive by disputing the concern; it survives by re-entering under the failure-mode-exposure category that the same guidance document names. A reviewer who applies the concern blindly will still reject; a reviewer who applies the concern and reads §1 will find the concern *pre-empted*.

**Check 7 — Reproducibility-without-unique-data**: PASS (on method). The method (crosswalk residual + pre-outcome Murphy decomposition + investigation-level CV + logistic classifier) is reproducible on any corpus meeting the three preconditions, without access to the 22-investigation corpus. The *empirical results* require the 22-investigation corpus or a successor corpus, and the draft marks every uncomputed magnitude explicitly. The split — method reproducible without the unique data, results corpus-dependent — is the clean-version of the check, and the draft states it cleanly.

### Honest bottom-line

Given the reframed Y, the C-scores and the 7-check scores *do* cross the first-class-paper threshold at the **ACL/EMNLP Findings level** (the honest ceiling stated in the draft), and *partially* cross at the main-conference level. The lift from the original framing is substantial: C1/C2/C3/C4 move from FAIL to PASS or marginal-PASS, C5 retains its PARTIAL with a sharpened method/results split, and the F4 7-check moves from 5/7 FAIL to 7/7 PASS with one marginal (Check 5, transferability, which inherits the population-of-suitable-substrates limitation). The single honest weakness that remains is the *empirical magnitude* slot: the draft contains zero computed numbers, and the headline AUROC, the standard deviation, the precision-at-recall lift, $N_{gt}$ and $N$ are all `[PLACEHOLDER-NOT-COMPUTED-YET]`. The paper's survival as a *first-class paper* — not as a first-class draft — depends on the run that fills the placeholders producing an AUROC that is genuinely discriminating (a widely-cited informal bar is AUROC > 0.7 on within-corpus CV for a method-level paper). If the run produces AUROC in the 0.55–0.65 range, the paper is honest but marginal; if the run produces AUROC at chance after correction, the paper is an honest negative result that should be reframed as such — not pursued under the prediction claim. The recommended posture is therefore: **pursue the reframe, run the 4-FICV evaluation to fill the placeholders honestly, and let the empirical magnitudes decide between (a) submit as a Findings-track prediction-method paper if AUROC clears the discriminating-power bar, (b) reframe as an honest-negative-result paper if it does not, and (c) leave as exploratory follow-up only if the placeholders cannot be filled because the corpus cannot be re-adjudicated to produce the labels.** The reconstruction is worth the run; it is not yet worth the submission. Drop it only if the run is infeasible.
