# F2: Recent (2024-2026) methodological-framing first-class papers in adjacent spaces

## Findings

### [1] arXiv:2604.07236 — Agent harness methodology paper that externalizes harness layers to quantify the LLM's residual role rather than assuming it central (first author: Sungwoo Jung)
- quote: "The contribution is methodological: once harness layers are made externally measurable, the LLM's role can be quantified as residual rather than assumed central."
- url: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07236
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-04-08 (v1; v4 2026-04-28)
- confidence: high
- key_message: "Agent harness layers, once made externally measurable, reveal that most of an agent's competence resides in the harness — the LLM's contribution is a bounded residual, not the central driver."

### [2] arXiv:2605.03310 — Coordination-as-architectural-layer paper that uses an information-controlled prediction-market design and Murphy-decomposed Brier scores to separate calibration from discrimination across coordination configurations (first author: Maksym Nechepurenko)
- quote: "We argue that coordination should be treated as a configurable architectural layer, separable from agent logic and from information access, enabling architectural reasoning rather than only engineering productivity."
- url: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03310
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-05-05
- confidence: high
- key_message: "Coordination in multi-agent LLM systems is a separable architectural layer whose configuration leaves distinguishable calibration-vs-discrimination signatures even when aggregate Brier scores coincide — measured via Murphy decomposition on live prediction markets."

### [3] arXiv:2607.01661 — InfoDelphi: designed information asymmetry as the key enabler of genuine multi-agent belief revision, proved by an ablation that removing asymmetry eliminates deliberation gains (first author: Yuante Li)
- quote: "When all agents are given identical evidence, deliberation collapses into herding rather than genuine belief revision, leaving multi-agent systems little better than a single agent. We identify this as a fundamental gap and propose designed information asymmetry to close it."
- url: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01661
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-07-02
- confidence: high
- key_message: "Multi-agent forecasting deliberation only improves calibration when agents hold asymmetric evidence — identical-evidence deliberation is herding in disguise, and removing the asymmetry ablation destroys the gain."

### [4] arXiv:2511.07678 — AIA Forecaster: first system to verifiably achieve expert-level (human superforecaster) LLM forecasting at scale, via agentic search + supervisor reconciliation + statistical calibration (first author: Rohan Alur)
- quote: "The AIA Forecaster approach combines three core elements: agentic search over high-quality news sources, a supervisor agent that reconciles disparate forecasts for the same event, and a set of statistical calibration techniques to counter behavioral biases in large language models. ... To the best of our knowledge, this is the first work that verifiably achieves expert-level forecasting at scale."
- url: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.07678
- source_type: primary
- published: 2025-11-10
- confidence: high
- key_message: "Expert-level LLM forecasting is achieved not by scale alone but by a three-part methodology — agentic search, supervisor reconciliation, and bias-correction calibration — and an LLM+market ensemble beats market consensus alone, proving the LLM contributes additive information."

### [5] arXiv:2607.09921 — Merger-arbitrage forecasting accepted at ICML 2026: long-context LLM forecasting beats calibrated market-implied probabilities by 24% in Brier score via hindsight-guided reasoning traces and expert-guided context engineering (first author: Hinal Jajal)
- quote: "Our system combines expert-guided context engineering with finetuning on hindsight-guided reasoning traces derived from historical deals. ... our finetuned system achieves the best performance of any method we evaluate, reducing class-balanced Brier score to 0.151. This is 24% below calibrated market-implied probabilities, 19% below XGBoost, and 25-42% below frontier language models."
- url: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09921
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-07-10 (accepted to ICML 2026)
- confidence: high
- key_message: "In specialized long-context financial forecasting, LLM-based systems beat both market consensus and gradient-boosted baselines when supervised with hindsight-guided reasoning traces — the methodological lever is the supervision design, not the model scale."

### [6] arXiv:2607.09349 — Deceptive Grounding: a new RAG failure mode (entity-attribution failure) invisible to all existing faithfulness/hallucination/citation checks, with a detection methodology at 97% precision (first author: Cedric Caruzzo)
- quote: "A clinical RAG response can pass every automated check (zero hallucinations, near-perfect faithfulness, real citations) while presenting drug Y's clinical evidence as evidence about queried drug X. We term this deceptive grounding (DG): a failure invisible to faithfulness, hallucination, and citation checks because every claim is sourced from a real document, about the wrong entity."
- url: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09349
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-07-10
- confidence: high
- key_message: "There exists a class of RAG failure — entity-attribution mismatch — that is categorically invisible to every existing evaluation framework because the evidence is real and correctly cited but attached to the wrong entity; a new verification methodology is required to detect it."

### [7] arXiv:2511.13240 — Action-belief gap: static calibration is an insufficient predictor of agentic consistency; models with better calibration are sometimes less consistent in dynamic settings (first author: Arka Pal)
- quote: "We uncover a significant action-belief gap — LLMs frequently take actions that contradict their elicited confidences. ... Crucially, we show that static calibration is an insufficient predictor of consistency in the above dynamic settings, as stronger, better calibrated models are sometimes less consistent than their smaller and weaker open-source counterparts."
- url: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.13240
- source_type: primary
- published: 2025-11-17 (v2 2026-02-08)
- confidence: high
- key_message: "Knowing what a model knows (static calibration) does not guarantee it acts rationally on that knowledge — the action-belief gap is a distinct methodological blind spot requiring agentic-setting evaluation, not transferable from static benchmarks."

### [8] arXiv:2603.09891 — TREC 2025 RAG Track: a multi-layered evaluation framework that makes attribution verification a first-class evaluation dimension alongside relevance and completeness, across 150+ submissions (first author: Shivani Upadhyay)
- quote: "The track leverages the MS MARCO V2.1 corpus and employs a multi-layered evaluation framework encompassing relevance assessment, response completeness, attribution verification, and agreement analysis."
- url: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09891
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-03-10
- confidence: high
- key_message: "Attribution verification — checking that every generated claim traces to a specific retrieved passage — is formally elevated to a first-class evaluation axis in the TREC 2025 RAG track, moving the community from faithfulness-only to provenance-grounded RAG evaluation."

### [9] arXiv:2603.00873 — MC-Search (ICLR 2026): first agentic multimodal RAG benchmark with hop-wise attribution and verification (HAVE), using step-wise annotated reasoning chains to evaluate process-level retrieval fidelity (first author: Xuying Ning)
- quote: "We present MC-Search, the first benchmark for agentic MM-RAG with long, step-wise annotated reasoning chains spanning five representative reasoning structures. Each example specifies sub-questions, retrieval modalities, supporting facts, and intermediate answers, with fidelity ensured by HAVE (Hop-wise Attribution and Verification of Evidence)."
- url: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00873
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-02-28 (accepted to ICLR 2026)
- confidence: high
- key_message: "Process-level evaluation of agentic RAG — verifying attribution at each reasoning hop, not just final answers — reveals systematic over/under-retrieval failures invisible to outcome-only benchmarks; the HAVE methodology makes intermediate evidence traceability the evaluation primitive."

### [10] arXiv:2605.27489 — HARP: a trace-first methodology for measuring harm amplification (local-to-global propagation) in multi-agent LLM systems, complementing attack-success-rate with a propagation metric (first author: Md Hafizur Rahman)
- quote: "We introduce HARP (Harm Amplification through Role Perturbation), a trace-first methodology for studying local-to-global harm amplification in multi-agent LLM systems. ... This complements attack success rate with a measure of how strongly orchestration spreads harm beyond the attack point."
- url: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.27489
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-05-26
- confidence: medium
- key_message: "Multi-agent LLM security evaluation must measure harm propagation (how strongly orchestration amplifies a local perturbation into system-level failure), not just attack success rate — the ratio H_global/H_local is a new methodological primitive."

### [11] arXiv:2606.17383 — POMDP-based model validation framework for agentic AI that decomposes autonomous decision-making into independently validatable components (belief-state, forecast, policy), extending model-risk management to autonomous systems (first author: Matthew Francis Dixon)
- quote: "The framework decomposes autonomous decision making into information, beliefs, forecasts, actions, and utility, allowing each component to be validated independently. ... The principal contribution of the paper is a practical framework for extending established model risk management concepts to autonomous AI systems."
- url: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17383
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-06-15
- confidence: medium
- key_message: "Agentic AI model risk cannot be validated as a monolith — decomposing the agent into a POMDP (belief → forecast → action → utility) and validating each component independently is the methodological bridge from predictive-model risk management to autonomous-system governance."
