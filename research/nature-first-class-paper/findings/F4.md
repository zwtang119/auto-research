## Methodological-insight test (derived from findings)

7 binary checks. For each, answer YES or NO:

- [ ] **Check 1 — Single-methodological-insight sentence test**: Can the paper's core contribution be stated as ONE sentence that expresses a methodological insight (a new method, a new evaluation protocol, a new reconciliation approach, a new failure-mode exposure), NOT merely "we built/collected X"? (Derived from Findings 1, 3, 14; Mensh single-key-message test from brief.md)
- [ ] **Check 2 — Removal-of-data-uniqueness survival test**: If you mentally remove the "our data is unique/first-of-its-kind" claim, does the paper still have a *methodological* contribution that survives? If the paper collapses without the data-uniqueness claim, it fails. (Derived from project D17 rule + Finding 1: "Datasets-as-endpoints don't meet the bar on their own")
- [ ] **Check 3 — Evaluative-claim articulation test**: Does the paper clearly articulate *what specific evaluative claims* the data/method supports, *under what assumptions*, and *what limitations constrain them*? (Derived from Findings 2, 4, 15)
- [ ] **Check 4 — Knowledge-gap test**: Is there a clearly stated *knowledge gap* in the existing literature that this paper fills, where the gap is about understanding/methodology, not just "no one has collected this data before"? (Derived from Findings 14, 5, 12)
- [ ] **Check 5 — Transferability-of-insight test**: Would a researcher in an *adjacent* subfield (not the same dataset domain) find the methodological insight useful for their own work? Or is the insight only useful to people working on this exact dataset? (Derived from Finding 3: insight must generalize; Finding 13: originality from analysis, not unique data)
- [ ] **Check 6 — Deep-concern resilience test**: If all reviewers agreed on a "deep concern" (e.g., missing baseline, methodological flaw, insufficient evaluation context), could the authors survive rebuttal by pointing to data uniqueness as a strength? NO — data-uniqueness cannot rescue a methodologically flawed paper. (Derived from Finding 8: "authors will not succeed by pointing out additional strengths if the deep concerns are true")
- [ ] **Check 7 — Reproducibility-without-unique-data test**: Could another team reproduce the *methodological insight* (not the exact dataset) using publicly available or easily collectible alternative data? If the insight requires your specific unique dataset to demonstrate, it may be data-driven gaming. (Derived from Finding 9: "inadequate reproducibility" is a reject reason; Finding 13: "Beating a baseline is not required" implies the insight is method-reproducible)

Apply to P1+P2 (dual-ledger crosswalk + orthogonal enums + Brier replay): 
- Check 1: PASS — the contribution is "a dual-ledger crosswalk methodology that maps free-form evidence entries to a structured factor ledger with orthogonal enumeration categories, enabling Brier-scored replay calibration of settlement predictions." This is a method, not a dataset.
- Check 2: PASS — removing the Gulei 2015 petrochemical scenario data does not collapse the dual-ledger crosswalk methodology; the crosswalk + orthogonal enums + Brier replay approach is methodologically independent of the specific scenario.
- Check 3: PARTIAL — the evaluative claim (Brier calibration of settlement predictions) is stated, but the assumptions/limitations need tighter articulation for first-class.
- Check 4: PASS — the knowledge gap is "no systematic reconciliation methodology exists for mapping unstructured evidence to structured factor ledgers with calibration-grade replay."
- Check 5: PASS — the dual-ledger reconciliation methodology transfers to any domain with evidence → factor → settlement pipelines (insurance claims, medical diagnosis, legal verdicts).
- Check 6: PASS — even under deep-concern pressure, the methodological contribution (crosswalk + Brier replay) survives independent of data uniqueness.
- Check 7: PASS — the crosswalk methodology and Brier replay can be demonstrated on any scenario with sufficient evidence-ledger structure.
- Preliminary verdict: P1+P2 PASSES the data-uniqueness-as-novelty rejection test. It is method-driven, not data-driven. (Defer final to orchestrator.)
