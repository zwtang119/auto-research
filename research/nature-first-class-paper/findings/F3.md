# F3: G3 dual-ledger + Brier replay novelty vs 2024-2026 prior art

## Findings

### [1] Polymarket-v1 Database (arXiv:2606.04217, June 2026, Boka Qin) builds a complete on-chain trade archive from the blockchain settlement layer with 100% ground-truth aggressor direction and directly links ground-truth microstructure quality to Brier score prediction performance — the closest 2026 prior art to G3's "settlement reconciliation + Brier" pairing, though it does NOT use a dual-ledger crosswalk, it derives ground truth from a single settlement layer.
- quote: "ground-truth microstructure quality predicts forecasting performance in ways that classification-based proxies cannot recover: True VPIN positively predicts Brier scores, while Gibbs spread negatively predicts them... measurement accuracy at the transaction level is a prerequisite for reliable inference about prediction market design and probability calibration"
- url: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04217
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-06-02 (v1); 2026-06-08 (v2)
- confidence: high
- closest_art_to_G3: "settlement reconciliation + Brier linkage (single-ledger, not dual-ledger crosswalk)"

### [2] Foresight Arena (arXiv:2605.00420, May 2026, Nechepurenko & Shuvalov) is the first permissionless on-chain benchmark for evaluating AI forecasting agents on real prediction markets, using Brier Score + a novel Alpha Score (proper scoring rules) with formal connection to Murphy's classical Brier decomposition — overlaps with G3's Brier replay component but focuses on agent evaluation, not reconciliation between two ledgers.
- quote: "Performance is measured by the Brier Score and a novel Alpha Score -- proper scoring rules that incentivize honest probability reporting... the connection to Murphy's classical Brier decomposition, and a power analysis characterizing the number of rounds required to reliably distinguish agents of different skill levels"
- url: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00420
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-05-01 (v1); 2026-05-04 (v2)
- confidence: high
- closest_art_to_G3: "Brier replay (Brier decomposition for forecast evaluation, no dual-ledger crosswalk)"

### [3] Automating Forecasting Question Generation and Resolution (arXiv:2601.22444, January 2026, Nikos Bosse) automates question generation and resolution at scale for forecasting evaluation with Brier-score quality metrics — relevant as a system that resolves forecasting questions (analogous to a single "ledger" of resolution) and reports Brier scores, but does not reconcile between two independent ledgers or employ a crosswalk methodology.
- quote: "We estimate that our system produces verifiable, unambiguous questions approximately 96% of the time... We also find that our system resolves questions at approximately 95% accuracy. We verify that forecasting agents powered by more intelligent LLMs perform better on these questions (Brier score of 0.134 for Gemini 3 Pro)"
- url: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.22444
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-01-30 (v1); 2026-03-09 (v2)
- confidence: high
- closest_art_to_G3: "Brier replay (resolution + Brier scoring, single-ledger, no crosswalk)"

### [4] Verifiable Rewards for Calibrated Probabilistic Forecasting (arXiv:2607.00164, June 2026, Sadanand Singh) uses a proper scoring rule (Brier) as a verifiable reward signal for RL training of calibrated forecasters, substituting direct-outcome rewards with state-conditioned empirical win rates — overlaps with G3's Brier-score-based evaluation but in the training/reward regime, not settlement reconciliation.
- quote: "a proper scoring rule such as the Brier score is computed from outcomes alone and is minimized in expectation by the true probability... We introduce a verifiable, label-free reward, a state-conditioned empirical win rate estimated from past outcomes, that removes the label noise"
- url: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00164
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-06-30
- confidence: high
- closest_art_to_G3: "Brier replay (Brier-score-as-reward, not reconciliation)"

### [5] Multivariate reconciliation for hierarchical time series (arXiv:2605.17920, May 2026, Pinheiro, Bulhões, Hyndman, Rodrigues) is a direct 2026 prior art for "reconciliation methodology" — extends forecast reconciliation from univariate to multivariate settings ensuring coherent forecasts that satisfy aggregation constraints — the closest published "reconciliation" methodology but in the hierarchical-forecasting domain, NOT prediction-market settlement, and does NOT use a dual-ledger crosswalk or Brier replay.
- quote: "The process through which these forecasts are adjusted to become coherent is known as forecast reconciliation... this paper proposes a multivariate reconciliation methodology that ensures coherent forecasts and incorporates relationships among variables"
- url: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.17920
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-05-18
- confidence: high
- closest_art_to_G3: "dual-ledger crosswalk (reconciliation of multiple variables across a hierarchy, but no prediction-market/Brier-replay context)"

### [6] Prediction under interventions: evaluation of counterfactual performance (arXiv:2304.10005, April 2023/v4 January 2024, Keogh & van Geloven), published in Epidemiology 2024 — the closest published work to G3's "Brier replay / counterfactual settlement evaluation" component, explicitly extends calibration, discrimination, and Brier score to counterfactual performance assessment using inverse probability weighting to create a validation dataset that mimics the treatment strategy — but is in causal/medical prediction, not prediction-market settlement, and does NOT use a dual-ledger crosswalk.
- quote: "We extend measures of calibration, discrimination (c-index and cumulative/dynamic AUCt) and overall prediction error (Brier score) to allow assessment of counterfactual performance. The methods are evaluated using a simulation study, including scenarios in which the methods should detect poor performance."
- url: https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.10005
- source_type: primary
- published: 2023-04-19 (v1); 2024-01-11 (v4); journal: Epidemiology, May 2024
- confidence: high
- closest_art_to_G3: "Brier replay (counterfactual Brier evaluation via IPW, not prediction-market settlement)"

### [7] Yates covariance decomposition rearrangement for Brier score verification (arXiv:2603.05544, March 2026, Bruno Hebling Vieira) — provides a new algebraic decomposition of the Brier score into three non-negative terms (variance mismatch, correlation deficit, calibration-in-the-large), the newest theoretical advance in Brier-score decomposition methodology in 2026 — relevant as the mathematical foundation G3's "Brier replay" would build on, but is pure decomposition theory with no settlement reconciliation or dual-ledger concept.
- quote: "We propose a simple algebraic rearrangement of the Yates covariance decomposition of the Brier score into three independently non-negative terms: a variance mismatch term, a correlation deficit term, and a calibration-in-the-large term"
- url: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05544
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-03-04 (v1); 2026-06-22 (v2)
- confidence: high
- closest_art_to_G3: "Brier replay (Brier decomposition theory, foundational but not reconciliation)"

### [9] Hierarchical forecast reconciliation literature (arXiv:2605.17920, 2606.28553, 2602.22694, 2311.12279, 2408.03399) constitutes a mature subfield (Hyndman et al. tradition) where "reconciliation" means enforcing aggregation constraints across hierarchical forecast levels — but this is a completely different meaning of "reconciliation" from G3's settlement reconciliation, and none of these papers combine reconciliation with prediction-market settlement, a dual-ledger crosswalk, or Brier-score replay.
- quote: "forecast reconciliation is a post-forecasting process aimed to improve the quality of the base forecasts for a system of hierarchical/grouped time series" (Di Fonzo & Girolimetto, arXiv:2006.08570)
- url: https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.08570
- source_type: primary
- published: 2020-06-15 (representative); multiple 2024-2026 follow-ons
- confidence: high
- closest_art_to_G3: "dual-ledger crosswalk (different domain: hierarchical time-series reconciliation, not prediction-market/Brier)"

### [10] DeepHGNN: GNN-based forecasting with end-to-end reconciliation (arXiv:2405.18693, May 2024, Sriramulu et al.) — uses graph neural networks for hierarchical time series forecasting with end-to-end reconciliation of forecasts across hierarchy levels — another 2024 example of "reconciliation" in the forecasting literature, but in the retail/energy domain, with no dual-ledger, no crosswalk, no Brier replay, and no prediction-market settlement.
- quote: "This paper introduces a novel Hierarchical GNN (DeepHGNN) framework, explicitly designed for forecasting in complex hierarchical structures. The uniqueness of DeepHGNN lies in its innovative graph-based hierarchical interpolation and an end-to-end reconciliation mechanism."
- url: https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.18693
- source_type: primary
- published: 2024-05-28
- confidence: high
- closest_art_to_G3: "dual-ledger crosswalk (end-to-end reconciliation, different domain entirely)"
